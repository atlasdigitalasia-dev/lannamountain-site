# SEO Audit: kickbackcoffee.co.uk
**Prepared by:** Lanna Mountain Marketing  
**Date:** May 2026  
**Audit Type:** Full Site Audit

---

## Executive Summary

Kickback Coffee is a well-positioned independent speciality coffee roaster in the UK, with a compelling brand story (Peak District roots, adventure/outdoor identity, 40+ person team, 4 cafes) and genuine differentiators. However, the website is significantly under-optimised for organic search — the indexed page count is very low (~10 pages visible to Google), blog content lacks commercial SEO intent, and there is no evidence of targeting high-volume transactional keywords like "coffee subscription UK" or "buy speciality coffee beans UK." The biggest strength is a strong brand narrative and multi-location footprint that could fuel powerful local SEO. The top three priorities are: (1) build out transactional landing pages targeting subscription and buy-intent keywords, (2) fix on-page title/meta optimisation across all product and collection pages, and (3) launch a local SEO push for all four café locations. The site currently has the brand but not the SEO infrastructure to compete against the likes of Batch Coffee, RAVE Coffee, or Square Mile.

**Overall assessment: Good brand, weak SEO foundation — significant upside available with moderate effort.**

## Competitor Comparison

Competitors are split into three meaningful segments — local café rivals, regional roasters (the real online/wholesale competition), and national subscription brands (relevant only for broad transactional keywords).

---

### Segment 1: Local Café Competitors (Altrincham, Bollington, Sale Moor, Macclesfield)

These are the businesses Kickback competes with for "coffee shop near me" and local Google searches. **None of them are roasters — Kickback has a structural SEO advantage here.**

| Competitor | Location | Web Presence | Local SEO Threat |
|---|---|---|---|
| Imperfect Coffee | Altrincham (near station) | Basic | Low — no roastery, no online shop |
| MOURO Coffee Bar | Altrincham | Basic | Low |
| Toast Altrincham | Altrincham | Some | Low |
| Café Waterside | Bollington | Facebook only | Very Low |
| The Green, Bollington | Bollington | Facebook only | Very Low |
| The Little Coffee Shop (new) | Bollington High St | Minimal | Very Low |
| Café/independents | Sale Moor | Minimal web presence | Very Low |

**Verdict:** In the local café segment, Kickback is the only player with a real website, a brand, and a roastery story. They should dominate local search for all four towns — but currently have no location pages to make that happen.

---

### Segment 2: Regional Roasters (Online Sales + Wholesale)

These are Kickback's real SEO competitors for "coffee roasters Manchester/Cheshire", wholesale B2B leads, and online bean sales.

*Note: Google's `site:` operator caps at ~10 results for all sites in this segment — not a meaningful differentiator. What matters is the quality and structure of those indexed pages.*

| Competitor | Location | Key Strength | Visible SEO Pages | Threat Level |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Ancoats Coffee Co.** | Manchester (Royal Mills) | Strong Manchester brand, wholesale, 10+ yrs | Homepage, roastery, coffee shop, wholesale, FAQs, location — mostly static, no blog, no ecommerce products visible | **High** — well-established Manchester roaster with similar B2B/retail mix |
| **Salford Roasters** | Salford, Manchester | Award-winning (Great Taste Awards), Shopify, online shop, subscriptions | Collections, subscription page, blends, wholesale, award-winning products — better ecommerce SEO structure | **High** — most SEO-capable competitor in the region |
| **Two Brothers Coffee** | Warrington | Multi-award-winning, subscription club, runs **cupping events in Altrincham** | Subscriptions, FAQs, events, products — solid structure; actively competing in Kickback's Altrincham backyard | **High** — direct local + online overlap; already in Altrincham |
| **The Little Coffee Roastery** | Macclesfield (same town as roastery) | Hyper-local, subscription-first, gift boxes | About, coffees, gift boxes, wholesale, packaging — subscription-focused, limited blog | **Medium** — same geography, but small scale; their subscription pricing (from £6.57) undercuts Kickback |
| **Dark Woods Coffee** | West Yorkshire | Adventurous brand, specialty wholesale | Trade/wholesale focused content — competes nationally for B2B terms | **Low-Medium** — wholesale keyword competition only |

**Verdict:** Ancoats and Salford Roasters are the primary online/SEO threats in Kickback's actual geography. Both have a head start but neither has Kickback's multi-location footprint or adventure brand angle.

---

### Segment 3: National Subscription Brands (Broad Keyword Competition Only)

These brands only compete with Kickback on high-volume national terms like "coffee subscription UK" or "buy specialty coffee beans online." They are **not** competing for Kickback's local or regional searches at all.

| Brand | Why They Rank | Kickback's Angle vs. Them |
|---|---|---|
| RAVE Coffee | Subscription-first brand, strong DA, dedicated landing pages | Kickback offers fresher, roastery-direct beans with a story |
| Batch Coffee | Discovery/curation model, review-driven content | Kickback is a roaster, not a curator — different product |
| Pact Coffee | Mass-market, VC-backed, huge domain authority | Not a realistic direct competitor — different tier |
| Square Mile | James Hoffmann halo, London-based, national reputation | Kickback's adventure brand is a genuine differentiator |

**Verdict:** Kickback can appear in national subscription searches but won't beat these brands head-to-head on DA alone. The play is to get into "best coffee subscription UK" editorial roundups via PR/outreach, not to outrank them organically.
